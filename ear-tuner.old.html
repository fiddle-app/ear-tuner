Ear Tuner
ear training for musicians

Ear Tuner measures your aural acuity — your ability to tell which of two notes is slightly higher or lower — and helps you improve it. The app tests you with notes that are closer and closer together, until you can no longer reliably tell which is higher.

The app remembers your best score for every note. Once you have a score for every note, it randomly selects notes to re-test, favoring notes where your score is weakest. You can manually re-test a specific note in the Scores table, below.

By practicing regularly, your aural acuity will improve.

Ear Tuner supports "eyes-free" operation: tap anywhere in the lower part of the screen to replay. Swipe right to continue after a wrong answer. In retest mode, swipe left to retry. Use the app with your eyes closed ("intonation meditation") or while driving (eyes on the road!).

The minimum difference that a person requires to distinguish between two notes varies. An untrained person may need 10–20 cents. An average musician might need 5–6 cents. A top-notch fretless string player may only need 2–3 cents.

Ear Tuner was inspired by the InTune app and Molly Gebrian's tips for improving intonation.
